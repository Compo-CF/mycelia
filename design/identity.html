Visual Identity / Field Guide v0.1 — 2026.06.29

Mycelia

A field guide to the visual identity of an atmospheric idle game about real fungi, slow time, and one small morel.

Mori — not a tutorial, not a mascot in the marketing sense. A small, ancient morel who stays in the corner of the screen and notices things. Speaks rarely. Pulses softly at a fifty-beat-per-minute heart rate when thinking.

I.Palette

Eight colors, named from the world they came from.

Two darks for ground and surface. Two earths for substrate. Two creams for type and fungal flesh. One bright accent for the night — used once, sparingly. One warning red — used almost never.

↑ Used everywhere Used rarely ↑

Hollow Black

The page itself. Not pure black — green-tinted, like the dark under canopy an hour before dawn.

#0B100D Page ground ≈ 50% usage

Moss Shadow

The primary surface. Slightly lifted from the page. Cards, panels, anything a finger can press on.

#1A211C Primary surface ≈ 20%

Lichen

Dividers, hairlines, the edges between things. Never a fill on its own. Always a quiet outline.

#2A332B Rules & edges ≈ 10%

Damp Bark

Substrate. Logs, stumps, the wet earth under the leaf litter. Where decomposition happens.

#3D2F25 Substrate / earth ≈ 8%

Old Leaf

Secondary text, captions, anything that recedes. The color of last year's leaves in February.

#7A6B55 Secondary text ≈ 5%

Spore Dust

Primary text and fungal flesh. A warm cream, not white. Calibrated so it never glares at night.

#D6C9A8 Primary text ≈ 5%

Foxfire Teal

Bioluminescence. The single bright moment in the game. Appears only when a glowing species fruits, or when Mori notices something rare. Never in chrome.

#5BC9B0 Bioluminescent accent ≈ 1.5%

Amanita

Reserved for the rare and the forbidden. A fruiting of Amanita virosa. A poisoned harvest. Two or three appearances in the whole game.

#8C2A2A Danger / forbidden < 0.5%

II.Typography

A naturalist's journal, set in three voices.

A characterful italic serif for names of things. A clean humanist sans for what the player reads. A small mono for what the game measures.

Bitter Oyster

Panellus stipticus · Mycenaceae

Pale brown by day. At night, the gills throw a steady green light, bright enough to read a thumbprint by. Common on wet hardwood. Found here from late summer through the first hard frost. Tastes of nothing in particular, but luminous to the eye.

IV.The fruiting moment

Most of the screen is dark. Then one thing is not.

The whole identity converges on a single composition: in the night biomes, a fruiting body throws Foxfire Teal into the dark. Restraint everywhere else is what makes that moment land.

Swamp · 02:14 ▲ 1,847 spores
Bitter Oyster
Panellus stipticus

What this composition encodes

  • No UI chrome. The species name fades in only because something is fruiting. When nothing is happening, the screen is just the forest.
  • One light source. The Foxfire glow is the only saturated color in the frame. Everything else is in the earth range.
  • Mori is small. About 8% of screen height, lower-left. Visible, but not the subject.
  • The species name is in italic display serif. The latin is in the body face beneath it. Specimen-label discipline, not game UI.
  • The number in the top-right reads in cream. Spore count is the only persistent UI. Tabular numerals. No icon ornament.
